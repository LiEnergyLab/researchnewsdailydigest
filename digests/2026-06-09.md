### [Regulation of the Charge Distribution in the Confined Channels of Anion Exchange Membranes Enables Efficient Ion Transport](https://doi.org/10.1002/aenm.71164)
**Score:** 10/10 · **Tag:** AEM membranes · **Journal:** OpenAlex · **Published:** 2026-06-07 · **Authors:** Shiqi Peng, Siwen Lu, Xi Zhang, Yuehua Chen, Li Zhang, Yiran Li

Placing charged groups in the middle of sub-nanometer channels—rather than on the channel walls—in an anion exchange membrane reduces excessive water uptake and forms a hydrogen-bond network more like bulk water, boosting ion diffusion. The resulting membrane (QPAEPi-3) achieves a chloride conductivity of 42 mS cm⁻¹ at 30°C and a 55% energy efficiency at 500 mA cm⁻² in a pH-neutral aqueous organic redox flow battery. This design strategy directly addresses the selectivity-conductivity trade-off by tuning charge distribution within confined channels.
