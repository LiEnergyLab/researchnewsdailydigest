### [Pulsed Electrolysis Promotes Catalyst Activity in Dilute CO 2 Streams](https://doi.org/10.1021/acsenergylett.6c00854)
**Score:** 10/10 · **Tag:** CO2 electroreduction · **Journal:** OpenAlex · **Published:** 2026-06-08 · **Authors:** Dania Muhieddine Orfali, Maya F. Schuchert, Ricardo Mathison, Amelia Rose, Justin C. Bui, Miguel A. Modestino

Pulsed electrolysis boosts CO₂ reduction selectivity from 25.6% to 78.6% and energy productivity from 0.77 to 2.59 mol kWh⁻¹ in a 25% CO₂ stream at 400 mA cm⁻², overcoming mass transport limits that normally plague dilute feeds. A multiphysics model shows pulsing raises CO₂ concentration inside the catalyst layer by 64%, explaining the improvement. The approach offers a way to directly convert variable, low-concentration industrial CO₂ streams without expensive purification steps.

### [How Does Water Dissociation Work in Bipolar Membranes?](https://doi.org/10.1021/jacs.6c05146)
**Score:** 10/10 · **Tag:** bipolar membranes · **Journal:** OpenAlex · **Published:** 2026-06-02 · **Authors:** Yifan Wu, Nathan Stovall, Dawei Xi, Shujin Hou, Prasad Sarma, Olivia T. Vulpin

Bipolar membrane water dissociation (WD) is accelerated by orders of magnitude when heterogeneous catalysts like metal oxides or graphene oxides are added, not just by the electric field alone. The catalysts work through a combination of surface acid–base chemistry and redistribution of the junction’s electric potential, creating two distinct kinetic regimes. The field still lacks predictive models because key local variables—pH, potential, and catalyst charge state—remain unmeasured.
