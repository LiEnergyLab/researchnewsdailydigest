### [Alkali metal cation impurities stabilize Cu + in oxide-derived copper for CO 2 electrolysis](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41929-026-01571-2)
**Score:** 10/10 · **Tag:** CO2 electrolysis · **Journal:** Nature Catalysis · **Published:** 2026-07-06 · **Authors:** Chang Hyuck Choi

Alkali metal cations from the electrolyte become trapped inside oxide-derived copper during CO₂ electrolysis, and cryogenic atom probe tomography shows that these trapped impurities stabilize Cu⁺ species, which correlate with higher activity for making multicarbon products. This suggests the catalytic role of oxide-derived copper is partly due to electrolyte-derived cation incorporation, not just the copper oxide itself.

Two specialized working electrode assemblies enabled operando XPS and NEXAFS to track Ir and Ru oxidation states and oxygen species in OER catalysts. ### [Electrolyte Effects in Ni–N–C Single-Metal-Site-Catalyzed Electrochemical CO 2 Reduction](https://doi.org/10.1021/acscatal.6c02673)

In TMAHCO₃ electrolyte, a Ni–N–C catalyst achieves a CO partial current density of 25 mA cm⁻² at –0.7 V RHE, outperforming Cs⁺-based electrolytes.
