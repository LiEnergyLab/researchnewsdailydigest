### [Non-Monotonic CO 2 Reduction Selectivity under High Pressure: From Concentration-Driven Regime to Charge-Transfer Regulation](https://doi.org/10.1021/acscatal.6c00443)
**Score:** 10/10 · **Tag:** CO2 electroreduction · **Journal:** OpenAlex · **Published:** 2026-05-15 · **Authors:** X M Zhang, Kazuyuki Iwase, Ryusei Takayanagi, Yusuke Hashimoto, Takaaki Tomai

Under high CO₂ pressure (up to 20 MPa), gold and tin catalysts show opposite selectivity trends: gold’s CO selectivity drops at 20 MPa due to coverage-induced electronic effects that destabilize the *COOH intermediate, while tin’s formate production keeps improving. This non-monotonic behavior shows that beyond simply boosting reactant concentration, high surface coverage alters charge transfer and intermediate stability, which can override concentration-driven gains. The findings identify a coverage-regulated regime that must be considered when designing high-pressure CO₂ electrolysis systems.
