### [Non-Monotonic CO 2 Reduction Selectivity under High Pressure: From Concentration-Driven Regime to Charge-Transfer Regulation](https://doi.org/10.1021/acscatal.6c00443)

Under pressures above 15 MPa, CO₂ reduction selectivity on gold drops due to a coverage-induced electronic effect that destabilizes the *COOH intermediate, while tin’s selectivity for formate continues to improve. Local pH gradients at the electrode–membrane interfaces, driven by salt ions in impure water, are a primary cause of voltage loss and degradation in bipolar-membrane electrolyzers. This work establishes local pH control as a key design principle for running electrolyzers on impure water feeds.
