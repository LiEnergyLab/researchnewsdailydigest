### [Computational Data: Investigating CO electrolysis on cobalt at elevated temperatures to produce C 4+ hydrocarbons](https://doi.org/10.11583/dtu.31253590)
**Score:** 10/10 · **Tag:** CO2 electroreduction · **Published:** 2026-04-23 · **Authors:** Riyaz Mohd, Alexander Bagger, Nishithan Balaji Chidambara Kani, Brian Seger

Using DFT and meta-dynamics simulations, this work shows that elevated temperatures on cobalt surfaces can make C–C coupling steps kinetically accessible for producing C₄+ hydrocarbons from CO, with binding trends and free-energy surfaces compared against copper. The accompanying Python notebook provides tools to analyze BEP relations, activation barriers, and solvation effects, helping identify which elementary steps become favorable under specific electrochemical conditions.

**Score:** 10/10 · **Tag:** CO2 electrolysis · **Published:** 2026-04-22 · **Authors:** Bradie S. Crandall, Gregory S. Hutchings, Feng Jiao

This paper identifies that scaling CO and CO₂ electrolysis from single cells to stacks introduces major performance losses from uneven gas distribution, temperature gradients, and flooding, which are not captured in lab-scale tests. ### [Hydroxide-Permeable Hafnia–Organic Interlayers Protect Ionomer from Oxygen-Evolving Anodes in Pure-Water Alkaline-Membrane Electrolysis](https://doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv.15002268/v1)
**Score:** 10/10 · **Tag:** AEM electrolysis · **Published:** 2026-04-21 · **Authors:** Minkyoung Kwak, Sharmistha Bhattacharjee, Dong Su Kim, Yang Zhao, Ryan T. Hannagan, Brith Milenia Rojas Mendoza

Inserting a ~50 nm hybrid hafnia–ethylene glycol interlayer between the anode catalyst and ionomer in AEM water electrolysis reduces cell-voltage degradation and suppresses cobalt dissolution, with only a ~70 mV voltage penalty at 1 A·cm⁻². The film’s hydroxide permeability allows it to physically shield the ionomer from the oxidative anode surface without blocking ionic transport. This provides a practical route to improving interface stability in pure-water AEM electrolyzers.

### [Non-Monotonic CO 2 Reduction Selectivity under High Pressure: From Concentration-Driven Regime to Charge-Transfer Regulation](https://doi.org/10.1021/acscatal.6c00443)
**Score:** 10/10 · **Tag:** CO2 electroreduction · **Published:** 2026-05-15 · **Authors:** X M Zhang, Kazuyuki Iwase, Ryusei Takayanagi, Yusuke Hashimoto, Takaaki Tomai

Under pressures above 15 MPa, CO₂ reduction selectivity on gold drops because high surface coverage weakens the electron transfer per CO₂ molecule, making the *COOH-to-*CO pathway less favorable; in contrast, tin maintains rising formate selectivity under the same conditions. ### [Electrochemical conversion of CO2 to formic acid on TiVC MXenes: A computational prediction](https://dspace.library.uu.nl/handle/1874/481075)
**Score:** 10/10 · **Tag:** CO2 electroreduction · **Published:** 2026-05-20 · **Authors:** Kamal Kumar, Archana Singh Kharb, Nora De Leeuw, Jost Adam, Abhishek K. Mishra, Geochemistry

TiVC MXene surfaces functionalized with –OH groups can convert CO₂ directly into formic acid (HCOOH) via a formate intermediate, bypassing the usual multi-step hydrogenation barriers. Pristine TiVC MXenes strongly chemisorb and spontaneously dissociate CO₂, but –OH functionalization shifts the reaction pathway to produce formate and then formic acid. This computational study identifies –OH functionalized TiVC MXene as a promising catalyst for electrochemical CO₂-to-formic acid conversion.

### [Impact of Pendant Amine Basicity on Electrochemically-Promoted Cobalt Hydride Formation: Kinetic and Mechanistic Analysis](https://doi.org/10.17615/yktj-p095)
**Score:** 10/10 · **Tag:** CO2 conversion · **Published:** 2026-05-16 · **Authors:** Montgomery C.L., Claytor Z.H., Dempsey J.L., Ertem M.Z.

The basicity of the pendant amine in a cobalt complex directly controls which proton-coupled electron transfer (PCET) mechanism forms the key cobalt hydride intermediate, with more electron-donating amines lowering the barrier for amine protonation. For the least electron-donating amine, one hydride-formation pathway is completely shut off. This finding provides a design rule for tuning ligand cooperativity to improve catalytic efficiency and selectivity in CO₂ reduction to formate and H₂ evolution.

### [Activation protocols for stainless steel in alkaline water electrolysis: trends and limitations](https://doi.org/10.20935/acadenergy8292)
**Score:** 10/10 · **Tag:** alkaline electrolysis · **Published:** 2026-05-15 · **Authors:** María José Lavorante

Stainless steel anodes for alkaline water electrolysis only achieve low overpotentials for the oxygen evolution reaction when activation treatments produce a nickel-rich oxyhydroxide surface layer; electrochemical activation offers the best balance of performance and processing speed. The review finds that inconsistent reporting of activation protocols across the literature prevents reliable comparison and identification of structure–property relationships. It calls for standardized testing and reporting to enable reproducible scale-up of these low-cost electrodes.
