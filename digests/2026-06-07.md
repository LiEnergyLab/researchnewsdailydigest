### [A hidden strain memory programs C2+ product branching in reconstructed copper electrocatalysts](https://doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv.15004349/v1)
**Score:** 10/10 · **Tag:** CO2 electroreduction · **Journal:** OpenAlex · **Published:** 2026-06-05 · **Authors:** Y N Wu, Zhe Zhao, Xin Wei, Yaru Sheng, Nannan Wang, Xinbo Gao

A machine-learning model trained on TEM images reveals that a specific type of lattice strain (εyy distortion) in copper catalysts acts as a "memory" that controls whether CO₂ electroreduction produces ethylene or C₂+ alcohols. The model predicts this product branching with high accuracy (R²=0.93), and experiments show that annealing erases the strain memory to favor ethylene, while tuning the catalyst's history can preserve it to boost alcohol formation. This work identifies catalyst history as a practical, programmable parameter for steering C₂+ product selectivity.

For fixed iridium loadings of 0.5 and 0.1 mg/cm², intermediate Ir contents of 30–45 wt.% in TiO₂-supported catalysts provide the best balance between electronic conductivity and catalyst utilization, outperforming both low-Ir (10 wt.%) and high-Ir (75 wt.%) formulations. Bipolar membrane water dissociation (WD) is accelerated by orders of magnitude when metal oxide or graphene oxide catalyst layers are added, not just by the electric field alone.
