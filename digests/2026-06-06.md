### [Atomic-Layer Thinning of Bismuth Oxide Confers Antireduction Stability and Tunable Protonation Pathway in CO 2 -to-Formate Electrocatalysis](https://doi.org/10.1021/acsnano.6c01383)
**Score:** 10/10 · **Tag:** CO2-to-formate · **Journal:** OpenAlex · **Published:** 2026-06-03 · **Authors:** Chang Wang, Shuxian Xie, Yanyang Qin, Jinwei Kang, Luyao Yu, Yanhong Li

Three-atom-thick bismuth oxide nanosheets maintain >90% formate Faradaic efficiency for ~50 hours at 200 mA cm⁻² by resisting reduction to metallic bismuth—only ~11.5% Bi⁰ forms at –1.0 V vs RHE, versus ~65.7% for bulk Bi₂O₃ at just –0.6 V. The atomic thinning introduces compressive strain that strengthens Bi–O bonds and shifts the rate-determining step, while also making bicarbonate the essential proton donor and creating a volcano-shaped selectivity dependence on bicarbonate concentration. This work provides a clear structural design strategy to stabilize an oxide electrocatalyst under reducing conditions and tune its protonation kinetics for industrial-scale CO₂-to-formate conversion.
