### [A hidden strain memory programs C2+ product branching in reconstructed copper electrocatalysts](https://doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv.15004349/v1)
**Score:** 10/10 · **Tag:** CO2 electroreduction · **Journal:** OpenAlex · **Published:** 2026-06-05 · **Authors:** Y N Wu, Zhe Zhao, Xin Wei, Yaru Sheng, Nannan Wang, Xinbo Gao

A machine-learning model trained on TEM images reveals that a specific type of lattice strain (εyy distortion) in copper catalysts acts as a "memory" that controls whether CO₂ electroreduction produces ethylene or C₂+ alcohols. The model predicts this branching with high accuracy (R²=0.93), and experiments show that annealing erases the memory to favor ethylene, while precursor and reduction protocols can tune it to boost alcohol formation. This work identifies catalyst history as a practical, programmable parameter for steering C₂+ product selectivity.
