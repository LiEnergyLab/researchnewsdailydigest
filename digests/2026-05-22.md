### [Electrochemical conversion of CO2 to formic acid on TiVC MXenes: A computational prediction](https://dspace.library.uu.nl/handle/1874/481075)
**Score:** 10/10 · **Tag:** CO2 electroreduction · **Journal:** OpenAlex · **Published:** 2026-05-20 · **Authors:** Kamal Kumar, Archana Singh Kharb, Nora De Leeuw, Jost Adam, Abhishek K. Mishra, Geochemistry

Using density functional theory calculations, the authors predict that -OH functionalized TiVC MXenes can convert CO₂ directly into formic acid (HCOOH) via a formate intermediate, bypassing the high-energy multi-step hydrogenation barriers typical of other catalysts. Pristine TiVC MXenes strongly chemisorb and spontaneously dissociate CO₂, while -OH termination shifts the reaction pathway to formate and then formic acid. This computational screening suggests -OH terminated TiVC MXenes are promising candidates for selective CO₂ electroreduction to formic acid.
