### [Metal-Dependent Regulation of CO2-to-Syngas Electroreduction in Cation-Exchanged SU-102.](https://doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv.15004048/v1)
**Score:** 10/10 · **Tag:** CO2 electroreduction · **Journal:** OpenAlex · **Published:** 2026-05-29 · **Authors:** Khaled Hassanein, Safwat Abdel‐Azeim, Ahmed Allosh, Patricia Horcajada, Munzir Suliman, Khaled Hassanein Sayed Ahmed

Post-synthetic exchange of charge-balancing cations in the anionic MOF SU-102 with Zn²⁺ or Ag species yields tunable syngas ratios during CO₂ electroreduction. The Zn-exchanged version produces a balanced, potential-independent H₂/CO mixture, while the Ag-exchanged version achieves 93% CO Faradaic efficiency at –1.4 V vs. RHE via in-situ formation of metallic Ag domains. This cation-exchange strategy offers a simple route to control syngas composition by altering the local catalytic environment within a stable framework.

Using low loadings of noble metals like Pt, Pd, Ru, or Ir to promote transition-metal catalysts significantly lowers the voltage needed for hydrogen production by replacing the slow oxygen evolution reaction with faster small-molecule oxidation reactions.
