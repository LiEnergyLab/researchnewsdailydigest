### [Linking wind-power fluctuation to degradation of PEM water electrolyzer](https://doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv.15006106/v1)
**Score:** 10/10 · **Tag:** PEM electrolyzer degradation · **Journal:** OpenAlex · **Published:** 2026-07-15 · **Authors:** Jiawei Yang, Yifan Tang, Rui Chen, Zhen Zeng, Tianyou Wang, Zhizhao Che

Faster replay of wind-power fluctuations causes more performance degradation in PEM electrolyzers than slower fluctuations, but dynamic operation is still less damaging than steady high-current operation. The degradation is driven by increased charge-transfer resistance, with rapid fluctuations promoting surface reconstruction to Ir³⁺-rich states, while steady high current causes more iridium dissolution and migration. A two-stage model based on these mechanisms predicts annual degradation under real wind profiles with just 0.6% error.

Ampere-level hydrogen production from commercial diesel exhaust fluid is feasible in an anion exchange membrane electrolyzer, but intermittent operation with continuous electrolyte flow causes more degradation than constant current operation. MIP-212(Al/Cu) achieves a benchmark CO₂ uptake of 2.30 mmol g⁻¹ at 0.15 bar and 298 K with a CO₂/N₂ selectivity of ~30, but its copper open metal sites make it highly hydrophilic, reducing performance in humid conditions.
