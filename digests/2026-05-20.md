**Score:** 10/10 · **Tag:** PEM electrolysis · **Journal:** ACS Energy Letters · **Published:** 2026-04-23 · **Authors:** Xue Yang

This review maps the key performance and durability gaps between lab-scale PEM electrolyzer tests (typically <5 cm² cells, <1000 h) and the multi-cell stacks (>250 cm², >20,000 h) required for industrial green hydrogen production. ### [Local pH control for impure-water-fed bipolar-membrane electrolyzers](https://doi.org/10.26434/chemrxiv.15002266/v1)
**Score:** 10/10 · **Tag:** bipolar membrane electrolysis · **Journal:** OpenAlex · **Published:** 2026-04-21 · **Authors:** Sanghwi Han, Gwan Hyun Choi, W Zhang, Dawei Xi, Duha Syar, Jaehyuk Shim

Local pH gradients at the electrode–membrane interfaces, not just bulk electrolyte effects, are a primary cause of voltage loss and degradation in bipolar-membrane electrolyzers fed with salt-containing water. Integrating the cathode and anode catalysts directly onto the membrane layers via spray coating and electrodeposition minimizes these gradients, achieving stable operation at 0.50 A cm⁻² and 2.8 V with a degradation rate of only 5.5 mV h⁻¹ over 120 hours in 0.50 M NaCl—a 13-fold improvement over conventional porous transport layer designs. This work establishes local pH control as a key design principle for durable impure-water electrolysis.
