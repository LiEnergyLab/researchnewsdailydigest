### [One-Pot Electrochemical Synthesis of (Hydroxyamino)methanesulfonate from CO 2 , Nitrate/Nitrite, and Sulfite](https://doi.org/10.1021/jacs.6c00509)
**Score:** 10/10 · **Tag:** CO2 electroreduction C-N coupling · **Journal:** OpenAlex · **Published:** 2026-05-30 · **Authors:** Aimin Li, Mengjie Liu, Hsiwen Wu, Haoming Yu, Ke Luo, Y Zhang

This work demonstrates the first one-pot electrochemical synthesis of a molecule containing both C–N and C–S bonds—specifically (hydroxyamino)methanesulfonate—by co-reducing CO₂ with nitrate/nitrite and sulfite on a cobalt phthalocyanine catalyst. The process achieves a maximum faradaic efficiency of 5.8% and a partial current density of −2.4 mA cm⁻², with mechanistic evidence showing C–N coupling precedes C–S bond formation. This expands the scope of multicomponent coupling in CO₂ electroreduction beyond single heteroatom products.

Post-synthetic exchange of charge-balancing cations in the anionic MOF SU-102 with Zn²⁺ or Ag species yields tunable syngas ratios during CO₂ electroreduction.
