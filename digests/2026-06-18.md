### [Charge-Consistent Estimation of Hydrogen Production in a Membraneless Alkaline Water Electrolyzer Using Time-Resolved Current Measurements](https://doi.org/10.3390/app16126073)
**Score:** 10/10 · **Tag:** alkaline water electrolysis · **Journal:** OpenAlex · **Published:** 2026-06-16 · **Authors:** Davut Sevim, Muhammed Yusuf Pi̇lati̇n, Serdar Ekinci, Erdal Akin

By combining continuous current measurements with end-of-test mass loss data, this study shows that hydrogen production in a membraneless alkaline electrolyzer can be accurately reconstructed using Faraday’s law, provided the calculation is constrained to match the total gas volume observed experimentally. The authors tested 13 KOH concentrations and found that both current and gas output depend nonlinearly on electrolyte strength. Two phenomenological models—a static polynomial and a semi-empirical grey-box approach—both achieved high internal consistency with the experimental data.
