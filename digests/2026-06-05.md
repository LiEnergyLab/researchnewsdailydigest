### [Stabilization of Unconventional Body‐Centered Tetragonal Phase in Copper Nanowires for Efficient Carbon Dioxide Electroreduction to Multi‐Carbon Products](https://advanced.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/adma.73600?af=R)

### [Atomic-Layer Thinning of Bismuth Oxide Confers Antireduction Stability and Tunable Protonation Pathway in CO 2 -to-Formate Electrocatalysis](https://doi.org/10.1021/acsnano.6c01383)
**Score:** 10/10 · **Tag:** CO2 electroreduction · **Journal:** OpenAlex · **Published:** 2026-06-03 · **Authors:** Chang Wang, Shuxian Xie, Yanyang Qin, Jinwei Kang, Luyao Yu, Yanhong Li

Three-atom-thick bismuth oxide nanosheets suppress reduction to metallic bismuth (only ~11.5% Bi⁰ at –1.0 V vs. RHE, versus ~65.7% for bulk at –0.6 V) and maintain >90% formate Faradaic efficiency for ~50 hours at 200 mA cm⁻². The atomic thinning introduces compressive strain that strengthens Bi–O bonds and shifts the rate-determining step from the initial proton-coupled electron transfer to the subsequent *OCHO protonation, with HCO₃⁻ identified as the essential proton donor. This work provides a clear structural strategy to stabilize an oxide electrocatalyst under reducing conditions while tuning the protonation pathway for selective CO₂-to-formate conversion.
