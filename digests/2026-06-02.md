### [Can dents and gouges compromise the structural integrity of hydrogen transport pipelines?](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.31560v1)
**Score:** 10/10 · **Tag:** hydrogen transport · **Journal:** arXiv · **Published:** 2026-05-29 · **Authors:** R. Das, B. Bezensek, E. Martínez-Pañeda

Hydrogen exposure does not significantly worsen damage from dents and gouges in pipeline steel unless the hydrogen is completely trapped at the outer surface of a pressurized pipe with a pre-existing passive dent and gouge. A new model combining hydrogen transport, plasticity, and damage mechanics was validated against experiments on X65 steel and used to simulate both passive and active defect scenarios. The key finding is that repurposing natural gas pipelines for hydrogen may be structurally safe for most common external defects, with the critical exception of fully sealed passive gouges under internal pressure.
