### [Pulse electrodeposition of Ni on Ti substrates for hydrogen evolution across acidic, alkaline, and neutral electrolytes](https://doi.org/10.1007/s44373-026-00149-8)

Optimized pulse electrodeposition of nickel onto titanium (8 pulses at -5 mA cm⁻² for 120 s) produces a nanostructured electrode that performs comparably to commercial Ni mesh for the hydrogen evolution reaction across acidic, alkaline, and neutral electrolytes. The Ni/Ti electrode achieves Tafel slopes of 149, 151, and 329 mV dec⁻¹ in acid, base, and neutral media, respectively, with the high neutral-pH slope attributed to mass-transport limitations from both proton supply and water dissociation. Using inappropriate normalization methods for electrocatalytic rates has led to incorrect conclusions about catalyst performance in several important reactions.
